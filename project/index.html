Biennial Report 2022–2023

  • COVID-19 Vaccination Efforts: Over 28 million vaccine doses were administered, with more than 13 million individuals fully vaccinated by December 31, 2023
  • Digital Infrastructure Support: The WHO provided 30 desktop computers, 30 uninterruptible power supplies (UPS), and 10 laptops to the National Institute of Public Health (INHP) branches across the country. Additionally, 75 laptops were donated to the Expanded Program on Immunization (EPI) officers in 75 health districts, and 759 tablets were distributed to Primary Healthcare Facilities (ESPC) across 56 districts in 16 health regions.
  • Capacity Building Initiatives: A total of 210 regional trainers and 1,072 ESPC staff were trained. The WHO also supported the training of 250 surveillance focal points, pediatric clinicians, and health district staff in nine targeted districts on disease surveillance.
  • Health Communication and Outreach: The WHO facilitated 16,511 radio programs in French and local languages through 175 community radios, reaching a wide audience with health information

WHO–Côte d’Ivoire Cooperation Strategy (CCS) 2022–2025

  • Strengthening the Health System: Improve health governance, access to essential medicines, health financing, and health information systems.
  • Promoting the Quality of Health Interventions: Focus on well-being throughout the life course, with particular attention to women, newborns, children, adolescents, youth, and the elderly.
  • Combating Diseases and Violence: Give special attention to the social determinants of health and the effects of climate change.
  • Enhancing Emergency Preparedness and Response: Build a resilient health system capable of responding to public health emergencies and disasters.

Malaria remains a significant public health challenge in Côte d'Ivoire, with an incidence of 268 cases per 1,000 inhabitants in 2023. While mortality has decreased by over 50% since 2017, the disease continues to affect vulnerable populations

Polio remains a concern in Côte d’Ivoire due to vaccine-derived virus strains. In 2024, over 9 million children were vaccinated in nationwide campaigns to stop the spread.

Learn more

As of 2019, approximately 76% of children in Côte d'Ivoire received all three doses of the diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis (DTP3) vaccine. Measles vaccination coverage for children aged 12–23 months was 70% in 2020. These figures indicate progress but also highlight areas needing improvement
